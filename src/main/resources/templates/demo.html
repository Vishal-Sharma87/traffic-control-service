How It Works

Animated walkthrough of the job lifecycle — submission into the priority queue, worker execution with heartbeat liveness, and fault recovery via dual schedulers. Each section plays independently.

01

Job Submission

Client submits a job → JobService attempts a semaphore tryAcquire() → if capacity is available, the job is inserted into a Redis ZSET with a priority score. Lower score = higher priority. Watch how a PAID job arriving after PUBLIC jobs instantly claims position #1 without interrupting the worker.

02

Worker Processing

A scheduled JobWorker polls the ZSET, pops the top job with ZPOPMIN, and begins processing. Throughout execution, it writes a heartbeat timestamp into a separate ZSET so the recovery scheduler can detect crashes. On completion the result is persisted to MySQL and the semaphore slot is released.

03

Recovery & Fault Handling

Two background schedulers guard against silent failures. HeartbeatRecoveryScheduler detects workers that stopped pulsing (crash / OOM) and rescues their jobs into a retry queue. RetryScheduler then inspects each rescued job — re-enqueuing it if retries remain, or routing it to the Dead Letter Queue when exhausted. No job is ever silently lost.
